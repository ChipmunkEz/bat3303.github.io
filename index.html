WELCOME TO BUSINESS ANALYTICS

Course BAT-3303-1 (46177) at Trinity University

Business Analytics and Data Driven Decision Science are the key elements in modern business. Are you ready?

Over the past few years we’ve seen that (big)-data analytics have become more and more important for businesses. Today most of us have contact with the results daily when we use google, buy a book on amazon, get a coupon attached to the receipt when buying at Target, etc. The uses of (big)-data analytics in a modern business setting include personalized marketing, recommendation systems, customer segmentation, cost benefit analysis, etc.

This course will be an introduction to applying statistics using R to solve business problems. We will explore problems that are frequently encountered in the modern business arena and that require a sophisticated degree of quantitative and analytical skills.

Course

Course Objectives

At the end of the semester you will

  1. be familiar with how to use quantitative methods to solve modern business problems that are data or calculation intensive
  2. have gained an understanding of the advanced statistical tool set ‘R’ to address quantitative problems found across business functions
  3. be able to use different data management methods, prepare data for analysis, and perform data analyses including data mining, linear and non-linear optimization, network analysis (among others) and
  4. know how to interpret the quantitative results and be able to explain to non-specialists the computational and methodological underpinnings of complex business systems modeling

Learning Methodology

In order to achieve this goal, we will form teams that formulate research questions and analyze these questions with real world data sets. We will start a topic by looking at the statistical fundament, then conceptualize and model the problem, and finally implement a solution using R, Shiny and MultiMarkdown.

Grade Composition

The student grade for this class will be computed as following

Part Percentage
Dashboard Development 30%
Project & presentation 45%
R exercise & book report 20%
Class participation 5%

This class will not have classical midterms and exams but rather will grade project and problem solving outcomes at the end of each section. In addition, we will have two projects that we are working on during the semester. The first will be the development of a dashboard around the middle of the semester, and the second will be a business analytics report for a San Antonio company at the end of the semester.

Dashboard development
The dashboard will be a product that you develop during the course with your team that represents important key performance indicators as well as realtime analytics functions.
R exercises
We will heavily use R in the class. At the beginning of each section you will get a problem that you need to answer by the end of the section using the tools and functions that you will learn in this section (and that you have learned so far). The exercises will be announced in class.
Book report
A 2-4 page report (font size 12, single space) of the book Naked Statistics, summarizing the main ideas of each chapter.
Project
Students will work in teams to analyze data sets using the statistical techniques and technologies studied in the course, prepare a report, and present their findings to the class and representatives of the company at the end of the semester. The company representatives will grade your work and it will have a weight of 50% on your grade.
Participation
In order to obtain full participation marks, a student must both be present and participate meaningfully in class. Students are expected to arrive on time for classes. Tardiness will impact the student’s participation grade without exceptions. The student’s overall participation effort will be classified into quintiles 1 to 5 by frequency of participation. Quintile 1 gets a raw participation score of 20% and quintile 5 of 100%. The raw participation score is then affected by the % of sessions the student was present, but if the student was absent 5 or more sessions the total participation grade is automatically zero. The only excused absences will be University-sanctioned events such as participation in varsity sports games or field trips, and up to two medically justified absences. The attendance will be recorded in each class. Participation is expected in a professional, respectful way.

For a passing grade in the course, the dashboard and the report must be developed in time. Failing to do so without pre-arranging alternatives with me will automatically mean a final grade of F. The projects have a hard deadline, meaning that work can't be submitted after this time.

Miscellaneous

Use of Cell Phones and Computers in Class

Please refrain from using cell phones in class. Cell phones should be silenced and out of sight. If you must answer a phone call or send a text message, please exit the classroom quietly. Laptop computers with the required software tools are available in the classroom and should only be used to take notes and complete exercises. Any other use of classroom computers, including but not limited to browsing the Internet, messaging, or writing personal email, is not permitted. Personal laptop computers and wireless devices are not allowed.

Academic Integrity

All students are covered by a policy that prohibits dishonesty in academic work. Under the Honor Code, a faculty member will (or a student may) report an alleged violation to the Academic Honor Council. It is the task of the Council to investigate, adjudicate, and assign a punishment within certain guidelines if a violation has been verified. Students who are under the Honor Code are required to pledge all written work that is submitted for a grade: “On my honor, I have neither given nor received any unauthorized assistance on this work” and their signature. The pledge may be abbreviated “pledged” with a signature.
